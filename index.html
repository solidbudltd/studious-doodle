SolidBud Ltd designs and delivers modular homes built from Structural Insulated Panels — the most precise, energy-efficient and structurally superior building system available today. We control every stage: from panel specification to the final sealed connection on site.

The Material

What is inside a SIP panel

A Structural Insulated Panel is not simply insulation with boards glued on. It is a precision-engineered composite structural element — three layers bonded under controlled factory pressure, forming a panel with predictable, verified mechanical and thermal properties.

  • OSB / 3
    Structural facing — OSB/3

    Oriented Strand Board forms both panel skins. CNC-routed to exact dimensions, it carries structural loads and accepts fasteners consistently across the entire face.

  • EPS / PUR
    Insulating core — EPS or Polyurethane foam

    Expanded Polystyrene or Polyurethane foam bonded to both OSB faces with structural adhesive under controlled pressure. No voids. No delamination. No inconsistencies.

  • OSB / 3
    Second structural facing — OSB/3

    Mirroring the first skin. Together the two OSB faces and the foam core create a stressed-skin composite that is stronger than its individual components.

How We Build

Precision at every stage of assembly

A SIP panel is only as good as the hands that assemble it. At SolidBud we treat the construction site as an extension of the factory — every joint sealed, every connection verified, every panel placed exactly where engineering demands it.

01

Foundation & sole plate

The foundation is prepared to exact level tolerances — slab-on-grade, strip footing or micropile system. The sole plate is set and laser-levelled before any panel is lifted. A perfectly level base is the non-negotiable starting point of every SolidBud build.

02

Floor panel placement

SIP floor panels are positioned on the sole plate, joints sealed with expanding foam tape and structural adhesive. Each panel is checked for level and square before fixing. The floor deck becomes the precision reference plane for everything above — no deviation is accepted.

03

Wall panel erection & connection

Wall panels are lifted into position and connected via precision-machined timber splines. Panel-to-panel joints are sealed with low-expansion foam and compression tape. Corner connections are angle-checked and anchored to the floor deck. Each panel location is confirmed against engineered drawings before the next is raised.

04

Roof panel fitting & ridge connection

Roof SIP panels are placed to the pre-cut ridge beam. Ridge, eave and gable connections are sealed with structural adhesive and expanding foam. Every panel-to-panel joint on the roof receives an airtight tape layer — because thermal bridging and air infiltration begin at the joints, and at SolidBud they end there.

05

Airtightness sealing & service routes

Once the shell is closed, all penetrations — pre-cut electrical chases, mechanical service entries, window and door openings — are sealed and verified. Factory-routed conduit channels within the panels keep services clean and the airtight layer intact throughout.

06

Verification, blower test & handover

Every SolidBud modular shell is inspected against the full engineering package. Dimensional checks, connection audits and thermal envelope verification are documented. A blower-door test confirms the shell performs to specification. What we hand over is a certified, precision-built structure ready for finishing.
